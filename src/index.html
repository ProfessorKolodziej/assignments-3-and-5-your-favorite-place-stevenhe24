Harbin

Harbin, dubbed the "Ice City" and "Little Paris of the Orient," dazzles with its Harbin International Ice and Snow Sculpture Festival, featuring colossal ice castles and sculptures illuminated in vibrant colors. Beyond its winter allure, this northeastern Chinese city boasts a rich Russian-Chinese cultural legacy, with landmarks like St. Sophia Cathedral and the European flair of Central Street. The Songhua River's banks come alive in summer, offering a contrast to the winter's icy enchantment. Harbin, a year-round destination,promises adventure and cultural discovery.

Top Attractions

  1. Saint Sophia Cathedral

    The cathedral was firstly built in 1907 by Russian. It now houses Harbin Architecture Art Gallery. It is a perfect example of Neo-Byzantine architecture. Saint Sophia Cathedral is the largest Eastern Orthodox church in the far east with more than 112 year’s history. Inside of the church, there are thousands of photographs showing Harbin city’s past, present and future.

    A link with more information about the Cathedral: Saint Sophia Cathedral

  2. Central Street

    Built in 1898 by Russians, Harbin's Central Street was originally called 'Chinese Street'. It measures 1,450 meters long, and it is the longest pedestrian street in China, also known as 'the First Street in Asia'.This central avenue is a cobblestone street. Lined with lots of old European-style architecture, hotels, shops, and restaurants.

    A link with more information about the Central Street: Central Street

  3. Dragon Tower

    The Dragon Tower is a landmark telecommunications tower in Harbin. Standing at 336 meters (1,102 feet) tall, it is the tallest steel tower in Asia and features an observation deck offering panoramic views of the city. Completed in 2000, the tower is not only a symbol of modern communication but also a popular tourist attraction, featuring entertainment facilities, and an exhibition hall that showcases the region's culture and history.

    A link with more information about the Dragon Tower: Dragon Tower

  4. Unit 731 Exhibition Museum

    The Unit 731 Exhibition Museum serves as a solemn reminder of the atrocities committed during World War II by Unit 731 of the Japanese Imperial Army. This unit conducted horrific biological and chemical warfare experiments on prisoners of war and civilians. The museum, located at the original site of the Unit's headquarters, educates visitors about these dark chapters of history through exhibits of historical documents, photographs, and relics, aiming to ensure such horrors are never repeated.

    A link with more information about the museum:Unit 731

  5. Harbin Ice & Snow World

    Harbin Ice and Snow World, famous for its ice and snow sculptures, is one of the world’s largest theme park of ice and snow. It is the biggest highlight of Harbin Ice and Snow Festival. It features with so many exquisite ice and snow sculptures with different theme in every year. It is seen as “Disneyland of Ice and Snow”.

    A link with more information about the Ice & Snow World:Ice and Snow World

Harbin Cuisine

Harbin Sausage

If you go to Harbin, sausage with irresistible European flavor is sure not to be missed. This widely welcomed specialty is called Red Sausage because of its purplish red skin. They are cured meats made of the ingredients of flour, lean pork, a layer of casing, starch, salt, garlic and so on. Such a treasure can be found at almost every market and restaurant in Harbin.

Crispy Sweet and Sour Pork

Guobaorou, or known as Crispy Sweet and Sour Pork, is another famous member of northeastern style food. This dish uses a special sauce called sugar and vinegar sauce and mostly just a combination of sugar and vinegar. And you really do taste the sweet meeting the sour with this dish, as the vinegar mingles with the sweetness of scrumptious sugar.

Harbin Beer

Being founded in 1900, Harbin Beer is now one of the oldest beer brands in China. It has also become a symbol of Harbin. You can even find a giant ice sculpture of it inside the Ice & Snow World. The beer has a distinctive refreshing European flavor and tastes better when it is cold.

Travel Tips

  • Attend the Ice Festival in January: experience world-class ice sculptures, dress warmly; temperatures plummet below freezing
  • Try Local Cuisine: enjoy Harbin sausages, and local Northeastern style cuisine
  • Stroll Along Songhua River: Witness icy beauty. Ideal for daytime walks
  • Take cable car on Sun Island: taking the cable car, view the Songhua river and the city skyline
  • Morning Market Visit: Experience local life and fresh produce. Early start recommended.
